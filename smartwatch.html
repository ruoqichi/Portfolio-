Description

IAT 235 is a second-year program that introduces the theory and practice of designing visual representations of information. The third individual project is to design five original smartwatch interface; 3 standard concepts and 2 non-standard ones.

Allows user to control their Smart Home device. (Non-standard)
Allows user to see dogs around them, with the permission of their owners. (Non-standard)
Allows user to listen to music. (Standard)
Allows user to track their daily steps. (Standard)
Allows user create a To-Do List. (Standard)
Inspiration

Figure 1: Inspiration taken from here.

Figure 2: Inspiration taken from here.

Unlike usual smartwatch UI designs on the Apple watch, I wanted my designs to have an overarching colour palette. I took my inspiration from Figure 1 and 2, both these designs have a coordinating art direction. I believe this allows the user to have a more aesthetically pleasing experience while navigating through the smartwatch.

Sketching

Figure 2: Sketches for Steps application.

Figure 3: Sketches for the Music Player application.

Before moving to Adobe Illustrator, I sketched out various designs for each application (See figure 2 and 3). This helped me go through several iterations and find one that considered all aspects for the user.

Reflection

Although this was a small project, I very much enjoyed it and found it interesting to design for such a small screen. Unlike regular smartphone screens, when designing for a smartwatch one must consider the legibility of their design and how to deliver usability in such a small space and this project helped me learn of all these aspects.
